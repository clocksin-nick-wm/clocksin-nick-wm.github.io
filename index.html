West-Mec Start Annual Report

START

START began as a simple idea…create, help people, repeat. At its core, START is a collaborative space for people that care about the value of education and mentoring others. Through a variety of creative services, START blends education, business, and community to move ideas and people forward.

Facility Use

Between lab rentals, tours, and events START has served 3,324 people this year.

Camps

START summer camps are taught by industry professionals as well as through partnerships with youth organizations such as Bonanza Education® and Bricks4Kids®.

Students Served

What better way to learn about science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) than through playing with robots, coding video games and more!

Business Anchors

"There are no secrets to success. It is the result of preparation, hard work, and learning from failure."
Colin Powell

RIBYT

Based out of New York, this software development company created a cloud based “smart, digital concierge” for large scale events. The ribyt team gives back to education by serving on the advisory board for our West-MEC Coding program, ensuring they learn relevant code. Additionally, they teach mini-lessons to our high school students and have provided internships.

Youth4Youth

Larry’s story is compelling. In a room filled with teenagers, you can hear a pin drop. They hang on his every word. Larry Tracey, Director of Youth4Youth, is on a mission to provide leadership skills to our Valley’s youth and working with young people to bring about their own change within their community.

Primo Volo Aircraft Traders

New to START this year, Primo Volo Aircraft Traders provides a modern approach to representing aircraft buyers and sellers. They are also a valuable resource for our West-MEC Aviation Maintenance Technology program, connecting them with aircraft donations to give students more hands-on materials.

Student Achievements

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world."
Nelson Mandela

National Center for Women & Information Technology

1 of our students won the 2016 Arizona Affiliate Award for the National Center for Women & Information Technology (NCWIT)

Code Day

Code Day is a 24 hour coding event where teams of students work together to create an inoovative project. 4 students won awards at the Phoenix Code Day Event in February 2016.

FBLA within West-Mec Start

The first year of Start Coding showed the students ability to grow as a chapter to form an FBLA community. 11 students that went to FBLA regional competitions, were able to place. 5 students were chosen to represent our State at the FBLA National Competition in Georgia this summer.

Girls Leadership Conference

“Little girls with dreams become women with vision.”
- Unknown

West-MEC’s first youth leadership conference just for girls. Girls (ages 10-13) got to hear two phenomenal motivational speakers, set personal goals, learned speaking and listening skills, recognized limiting behaviors and created a craft with their personalized goal.

StartED

"You can discover more about a person in an hour of play than in a year of conversation."
- Plato

START.ed is a series of interactive classes in our Create Lab where a small group of individuals create in a casual setting. These classes allow people to gain confidence in using the Create lab equipment by having them make and take home a product.
